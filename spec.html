HTML6 – When and If?

The main goal with the release of HTML5 was to empathize the semantics, or the meaning behind each HTML tag. Another strong objective was to separate the context of a webpage with its underlying decorative blueprints. This means that HTML5 aims to reduce overlaps between the responsiveness of JavaScript, design of CSS, and the words on the page brought about by HTML. These changes were necessary because of the ever–changing web and antiquated coding practices. With all that said, will there ever be a future update to HTML5? Enter the prospective HTML6 language.

Now, according to WHATWG (Web Hypertext Application Technology Working Group), there will be no HTML6. However, they have adopted a “living document” approach to updating the language. What this means is that HTML6 will most likely be a series of updates that will occur more frequently as new methods are sought out and implemented. One such update includes the introduction of a “native”modal window that HTML can use without the assitance of JavaScript. This new <dialog> element will work in conjuction with JavaScript to confirm user action.

Another new standardization is the <menu> and <button> elements that will create a list of items that the user can interact with. This will make it easier for internet user's who do not use a laptop or desktop, such as a phone, to pick and choose from a list of options.
